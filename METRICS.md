# METRICS.md

# Metrics Strategy

For Aethra, the single North Star metric would be:

## Qualified Audits Generated Per Week

A “qualified audit” means:
- a completed audit
- with realistic AI tooling inputs
- that results in meaningful optimization insights
- and optionally includes lead capture or report sharing behavior

This metric matters more than generic traffic or daily active users because Aethra is fundamentally a lead-generation and decision-support product rather than a daily-use SaaS platform.

A user does not need to use the tool every day for the product to be valuable. The real goal is generating high-quality audits from startups or technical teams that are actively evaluating AI tooling spend.

---

# Input Metrics

## 1. Audit Completion Rate

This measures:
Landing Page Visitors → Completed Audits

This is important because the onboarding flow is the product’s first major bottleneck. If users abandon the process before generating a report, the rest of the funnel becomes irrelevant.

A healthy early-stage target would likely be:
20–30% completion rate.

---

## 2. Report Share Rate

This measures:
Completed Audits → Shared Public Reports

This is one of the strongest signals that the product is genuinely useful. Users only share reports if:
- the output feels trustworthy
- the design looks polished
- the recommendations feel worth discussing

Because Aethra depends heavily on organic distribution and founder discussions, report sharing is critical.

---

## 3. Lead Capture Rate

This measures:
Completed Audits → Email Captures

This directly impacts the business value of the product for Credex. If users receive value from the audit but refuse to leave contact information, the product becomes harder to monetize operationally.

A strong target would likely be:
35–50% lead capture rate.

---

# What I Would Instrument First

The first analytics instrumentation I would add includes:

- audit started
- audit completed
- report shared
- PDF exported
- email captured
- consultation CTA clicked

I would also track:
- tool combinations most frequently selected
- average projected savings
- which recommendations appear most often

This would help identify:
- the most valuable user segments
- common overspending patterns
- which audit outputs are driving the strongest engagement

---

# Pivot Trigger

The biggest pivot signal would be:

## High audit completion but low sharing and low lead capture.

If users complete audits but:
- do not share reports
- do not export PDFs
- do not leave emails
- do not discuss the output

then the product likely provides insufficient perceived value.

A rough pivot threshold would be:

- report share rate below 5%
- lead capture below 15%
- repeated audits without downstream engagement

At that point, I would reconsider:
- positioning
- recommendation quality
- onboarding flow
- report usefulness
- target audience

rather than simply trying to increase traffic.

---

# Why These Metrics Matter

The product behaves more like:
- a decision-support utility
- a lead qualification system
- a shareable optimization tool

than a traditional daily-use SaaS product.

Because of that, metrics like:
- DAU
- session count
- time spent

are much less meaningful than:
- audit quality
- shareability
- lead intent
- consultation conversion potential

The metrics strategy is intentionally optimized around:
- operational usefulness
- startup engagement
- organic distribution
- business conversion quality
